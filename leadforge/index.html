Where the data comes from

Sourcing, in plain English.

Buyers pay $1,200+/yr for Clearbit and ZoomInfo because they need to know what's behind the data. Same standard here.

Company firmographics

Name, size, industry, founding year, HQ, and LinkedIn page are pulled from a combination of:

  • Public company websites (parsed for "About" / footer / structured data)
  • Public LinkedIn company pages
  • Public business registries (where applicable)
  • Open-data firmographic providers

Contact records

Decision-maker names, titles, work emails, and LinkedIn URLs are sourced from public-facing professional profiles and validated through deliverability checks before they're returned.

Each contact ships with a confidence score (0–1). Anything below the configured threshold is flagged so you can decide whether to use it. No fake-looking emails padded into your batch to inflate match rate.

LeadForge does not sell, store long-term, or remarket personal data sourced from your queries. Enrichment is performed per-request and scoped to the domains you submit.

What does LeadForge actually do?

You give LeadForge a list of company websites. It tells you who works there, what those people do, how to reach them, and what the company actually sells. That's it.

Imagine a spreadsheet of 500 dental-clinic websites with no names, emails, or phone numbers. LeadForge turns it into a clean spreadsheet of owner names, work emails, LinkedIn profiles, company size, and which prospects are actually a good fit. What used to take a VA a week takes about 90 seconds.

For developers: REST API, bearer-token auth, batched up to 50 domains/call, 20-step workflow runner, idempotent fulfillment. Swagger docs →
